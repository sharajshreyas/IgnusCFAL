Course Pedagogy:

  • Pre-Class Reading Each session where a new concept is taught will be preceded by a pre-class reading, where the student will have to read material from the books. This is communicated before the classroom session. Books are purchased by the student which he would be using for the entire two years. Through our rental scheme, students can use these books at half of the wholesale rate.

  • Just in time teaching (JiTT) Before each class, a 5 minute test shall be taken to -Make sure the students have read through the material -See the difficulties the student faces in understanding so that the content can be tailored accordingly -Students can see the change and improvement in their reading comprehensibility over time

  • Classroom Sessions -Videos shall be screened giving an in-depth understanding of the concepts and summarizing the pre-reading that the student has done -These videos shall be made by senior professors who are very experienced in teaching JEE advanced, and also have been teaching respective subject for long -Videos shall have animations/example videos for application based understanding and also easy visualization for students -Problems videos shall be aired for examination based preparation

  • Active engagement in classrooms The following activities (statistically proved) shall be conducted in each class during the video lectures for active student engagement and understanding. -Peer learning (developed by Prof. Eric Mazur, Harvard University) -Think-Pair-Share (developed by Prof. Frank Lyman, Maryland University) Also on the basis of feedback and research, more activities shall be planned for better involvement and understanding of students.

  • Clearing doubts A repository of FAQs for each topic shall be built. Student will be given access to watch these videos whenever a doubt arises. Whenever a new doubt outside the repository arises, it shall be communicated to the professor and a new video addressing the same shall be made and added to the repository Even after this if the doubt remains, a video conferencing session shall be scheduled (once a week) to clear the unclear concepts/problems

  • Post-Classroom session Facilitated group discussions shall be held where students sit together in groups of 5-6 and solve problems from the daily practice sheets given If at all, none of the groups are able to solve a problem, it shall be added in the doubt repository and written solution is given, or a video is aired the next class.

Assessments

  • Quizzes and Tests:

     Rigorous tests are conducted for understanding of concepts and for preparation of competitive exams. The performance of students is measured and feedback is given by collecting responses of the following. Pre-class reading comprehension tests In-class activity responses Post-class tests on conceptual understanding Weekly tests conducted amounting to 115 tests 30 Major tests (Schedule in appendix) Tests focused on Competitive exams 10 Full length JEE Practice Tests 5 Full length CET Practice Tests

  • Assessments to students

     Students get a high level summary to assess his performance with that of all learning centers, thus giving a much better idea of where he stands. A breakdown of topic-wise as well as concept wise performance to assess where the student is going wrong and rectify the same (sample in appendix) Continuous data is fed into the database, with questions classified by concepts and difficulty level, giving a continuous and all round analysis and comments to better student performance

  • Usage of data for content making

     The data shall be analyzed to check the understanding of concepts, effectiveness of activities within and outside the classroom If progress is good, we shall move forward, else remedial sessions shall be held to address the issues

Counseling Sessions:

There will be continuous feedback given to the students and parents from the facilitator. The role of a facilitator is to make sure students at no point of time lose motivation throughout the two years. Further there shall be more expert feedback given to the students by the Ignus team members Post exam guidance shall be given to help the students choose the right enginering college and branch. This guidance is given by the alumni of IITs, BITS and NITs
